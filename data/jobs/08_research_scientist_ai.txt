Job Title: Research Scientist – AI / Machine Learning
Company: DeepAxis Research
Location: Cambridge, UK (Hybrid)
Salary: £80,000 – £110,000 + publication bonus

About the Role
DeepAxis Research is a deep tech company spun out of the University of Cambridge. We work on fundamental and applied ML research, with a current focus on uncertainty quantification, out-of-distribution detection, and efficient neural architecture search. You will conduct original research, publish at top ML venues, and translate findings into production systems.

Key Responsibilities
- Conduct independent and collaborative research in deep learning, probabilistic modelling, or related areas.
- Publish papers at venues including NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, CVPR, ACL, and EMNLP.
- Implement research ideas in PyTorch and contribute to our internal research codebase.
- Collaborate with applied ML teams to transfer research advances into product.
- Mentor PhD interns and junior researchers.
- Present research at conferences, workshops, and industry events.
- Stay current with the rapidly evolving ML literature; lead internal paper reading groups.

Required Skills & Experience
- PhD in Machine Learning, Computer Science, Statistics, or a closely related field; or equivalent research experience with a strong publication record.
- Deep theoretical knowledge of machine learning: probabilistic graphical models, variational inference, Gaussian processes, or deep learning theory.
- Expert Python and PyTorch (or JAX) skills; ability to implement novel architectures from scratch.
- Strong mathematical foundations: linear algebra, probability theory, information theory, optimisation.
- Track record of publications at top ML conferences or journals.
- Ability to define and pursue research directions with limited supervision.

Desirable Skills
- Experience with one or more of: uncertainty quantification, Bayesian deep learning, meta-learning, continual learning, or neural architecture search.
- Familiarity with JAX and functional ML frameworks (Flax, Haiku).
- Experience with distributed training (DeepSpeed, FSDP, Megatron-LM) for large-scale experiments.
- Knowledge of hardware-aware ML: TPUs, custom CUDA kernels, flash attention.
- Connections to the UK academic ML community; collaborations with university groups.
- Open-source contributions to foundational ML libraries.

Research Culture
Publish-or-perish culture is not for us: we value depth over volume. Researchers have 20% self-directed time for moonshot ideas. We sponsor visa applications and relocation.
